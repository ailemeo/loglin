Getting hints. In this larger problem, it's hard to decide which sliders to move. So, let's get some automatic help! Check the "Show gradients" checkbox. Now each slider shows a hint about the best direction to move it. For example,

  • A short rightward (red) bar indicates that the log-likelihood will improve slowly as you increase that weight.
  • A long leftward (blue) bar indicates that the log-likelihood will improve quickly as you decrease that weight.

Try following the hints on the sliders to match the gray outlines. Easier?

Drag the triangle slider back and forth to make its weight very big and very small. As you do this, what happens to the hints on the circle and square sliders? Why?

Understanding the hints. Recall the two games from lesson 3. The hint on the slider can be interpreted as a hint for either game.

  • Log-likelihood game. Following the hint on the circle slider will increase the log-likelihood. That's because the length of the hint bar is the partial derivative of the log-likelihood with respect to the circle weight.

  • Matching game. Following the same hint will also improve the match between the expected and observed number of circles. That's because the length of the hint bar is (observed circles - expected circles).

These two lengths are the same. The homework handout shows that the formula for the partial derivative is simply (observed - expected).
