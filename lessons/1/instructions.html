Welcome! This interactive visualization will help you understand the popular technique of log-linear modeling. For a quick intro with the simple defining formulas, consult the second half of this homework.

The sliders below control the parameters ("weights") of a log-linear model. When you increase the circle weight, which filled shapes get bigger? Which ones get smaller?

One game is to try to match all 4 shapes to the gray outlines. You will need to use both sliders. A shape will turn gray if it matches well. It turns red if it is too small, blue if it is too big.

Your model is defining a probability for each shape, shown by the area of the shape. When the weights are 0, all 4 shapes are equally likely (probability 1/4).

But the empirical probability of solid circles is 1/2, because 30 of the 60 shapes in your training data are solid circles. This "target" of 1/2 is shown by the area outlined in gray.

When your model says that p(solid circle) = 1/4, then it incorrectly expects only about 15 of 60 shapes to be solid circles. The observed count of 30 and the expected count of 15 are contrasted in the solid circle box. You are trying to make them match. So fix your model by increasing the circle weight!
