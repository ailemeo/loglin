Remark. Did the format of this example surprise you? Why should different contexts have to choose among different outcome sets? Because different situations present different choices! Consider estimating p(parse tree | sentence). Each n-word sentence presents a choice among many possible parse trees—whose n leaves are labeled with the words of the sentence, so they are parses of that sentence only. So how can we parse novel sentences at test time? More generally, how can we generalize to new contexts and outcomes? Because a (context,outcome) pair we've never seen in training data may still have many features that we've seen in training data.
