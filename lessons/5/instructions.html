Until now, all of our features have been binary. fsolid(y) returns 1 if y is filled with a solid color, and 0 otherwise.

But below, we illustrate a feature whose value can be 3, 4, or 5. For example, fnumber_of_sides(striped square) = 4.

Point to that feature's slider with your mouse. As you see, the dataset has 420 sides altogether, so the average shape has 4.6 sides. But the initial model predicts that the average shape should have only 4 sides because it thinks that triangles, squares and pentagons are equally likely.

If you change the feature's weight to 1 (or -1), then what is the ratio of model probabilities among the solid triangle, solid square, and solid pentagon? Try it! How does your answer change as you vary the weight of the solid feature?
